Adobe's creative monopoly

As you know, Adobe has always had a small "part" on the internet. First it was
with Glorious Flash Player. If you've never played a flash game, what world do you
live in? Click games, Friv, 360 games, Good times... Then I believe it was (until today, actually)
with Adobe acrobat DC, which was the best PDF reader at that time. Today, Adobe is
best known for the creative industry, being the creator of Photoshop, Premiere,
Illustrator, etc... And I'm not going to lie, the products aren't bad. All are very
powerful, and somewhat simple. The problems are different.

Exorbitant prices

Adobe has ended the lifetime purchase service, and now, you have to pay about
300 reais PER MONTH to Adobe if you want Creative Cloud. Adobe did this for a reason:
They know their services are the industry standard, and people will pay Adobe whatever
they want for their products. Alternatives exist: GIMP, Da Vinci Resolve, Inkscape,
Kirita, all are quite capable, but the problem is that people who keep paying can't
or don't WANT to learn. And it's not like the commands are 100% different.


As this monopoly continues, the creative cloud will become more and more inaccessible, and we will run out of alternatives.
