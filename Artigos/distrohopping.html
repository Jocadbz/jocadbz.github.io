Distrohopping sucks

I am angry, sad and very angry. I may have wasted several hours on this thing I call "the addiction" more
mortal that exists: The Hopping Distro

What is Distrohopping?

Distro-Hopping is a term that refers to the activity or hobby of Linux users to switch
frequently between distributions, exploring them and trying out multiple distributions in a
try to find the one that best fits your needs.

I jumped from several to several distros, Arch, Fedora, Ubuntu, Pop! OS, Feren OS, among others.
What I wanted was a DE that was nice, light, and easy to use.
The Gnome interface is very nice to use, but it consumes a lot
XFCE consumes almost nothing, about 500mb of ram, ugly as hell, and customization takes hours.
The famous WM, like the i3 and the sway, are very light and don't need to be pretty, but they're not easy
to use.

I was in this beautiful impasse until I remembered that I had a distro that has everything I want:

The glorious Solus

The Budgie interface is one that combines everything I want. It is written in Vala (I think)
so it consumes much less than Gnome (Which is written in Javascript), it is much prettier than XFCE
and it is easy to use even for a Windows user.


Finally I found the cure for distrohopping. Better focus on my Github now.
